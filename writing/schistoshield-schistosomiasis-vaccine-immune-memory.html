New in npj Vaccines: SchistoShield® schistosomiasis vaccine induces functional immune memory across US and African populations

June 2, 2026 · npj Vaccines

By: B Tadesse

A new study I co-authored has been published in npj Vaccines, reporting that the SchistoShield® (Sm-p80 + GLA-SE) vaccine candidate induces functional immune memory responses in both United States and African study populations.

Schistosomiasis remains one of the most neglected drivers of chronic illness in tropical and subtropical regions. The parasite causes roughly 290,000 deaths each year, an estimated 250 million people are chronically infected, and a further 800 million live at risk of new or recurrent infection. Despite this burden, no licensed human vaccine exists.

SchistoShield® is among the leading candidates working to change that. It has now completed Phase 1 safety and immunogenicity trials in the United States and a Phase 1b trial in Africa. This paper analyzes the immune responses from those trials and shows that the candidate elicits functional immune memory across geographically and immunologically distinct populations — an important signal for a vaccine intended to protect the communities that carry most of the disease burden.

For me, this work sits squarely at the intersection that defines my research: generating rigorous clinical evidence for vaccines against diseases of poverty, and ensuring that evidence is produced with and for the populations who stand to benefit most. Demonstrating comparable immune memory in US and African participants is exactly the kind of equity-relevant evidence the field needs as the candidate advances toward later-stage efficacy testing.

Reference

Balkhi MY, Kalyanasundaram A, Arya A, Kifle DW, Mckinnon M, Krishnappa D, et al., including Tadesse BT. Schistosomiasis vaccine SchistoShield® induces functional immune memory responses in US and African populations. npj Vaccines. 2026. doi:10.1038/s41541-026-01501-0.
